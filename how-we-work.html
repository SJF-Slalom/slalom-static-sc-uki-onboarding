S&C UK&I culture principles

How we work together. These principles shape our ways of working as much as our capabilities do.

Intellectual curiosity

We enjoy thinking deeply about complex problems. Our ways of working create space for this, rather than crowding it out with process.

Generosity of expertise

We share knowledge across capabilities and geographies freely. Cross-capability collaboration is encouraged and recognised.

Constructive challenge

We disagree well. Debate is valued — but decisions are respected once made.

Client centricity with backbone

We care about client outcomes, but we also give honest counsel — even when it’s uncomfortable.

Shared commercial ownership

We all help ensure our business is well architected, well run and financially healthy. Financial responsibility is not something that belongs solely to leaders or Finance. Every team member contributes through how we manage our time, support pursuits, steward client relationships and make cost-conscious decisions, we treat the business as ours — because it is.

Investment in each other

We look for ways to engage and grow everyone in our teams. Mentoring, sponsoring and developing colleagues is part of the job — not an optional extra.

Rhythms of our business

Some of the regular connects for our S&C group. Cadence may change based on the wider Slalom UK&I meeting schedule.

2–3 / year. A longer meeting, often with a social element — updates on S&C and Slalom as a whole.

A consistent bench process

Whilst on the bench (not staffed on a project), all consultants are expected to be at a Slalom office at least 3 days a week, meet consistently with their People Leaders, and attend the weekly S&C bench meeting. Project staffing happens weekly, on Wednesdays. Accurate consultant data — availability date, skills — is invaluable in matching the right talent to the right client need.

Last 4 weeks of project

  • Investigate extension or follow-on work within the same client with your AE/Leads and PL
  • Ensure your Slalom CV is up to date for skills & capabilities
  • Work with your People Leader & Staffing Lead to understand opportunities

Day 1 on the bench

  • Receive an email about being on the bench + invite to that week’s S&C bench meeting
  • Ensure your “available date” is accurate in Salesforce and your me@Slalom profile is up to date

Weeks 1–4

  • Meet your People Leader to discuss your action plan and opportunities
  • Search open opportunities in me@Slalom and favourite them
  • Attend the S&C bench meeting; get involved in pursuits and internal briefs

Weeks 5+

  • All week-4 activities continue
  • If held for a specific geography, begin looking at the next geo tier (e.g. EMEA, Global)
  • Use the time to complete training and certifications
A handy checklist of bench activities lives in the Bench People Leader + Consultant Staffing Checklist (ask your People Leader for the latest version).
